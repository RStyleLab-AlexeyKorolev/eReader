HYDRATION

All athletes need to drink water before, during, and after practices and games. This is especially important on days when both temperatures and humidity levels are high. If a youth soccer player does not drink enough water, she could suffer from dehydration.

HOW TO MAINTAIN PROPER HYDRATION*>>

  • Before exercise, drink 16–20 full ounces within
  • the two-hour period prior to exercise
  • During exercise, drink 4–6 full ounces
  • After exercise, replace 24 full ounces for every one pound of body weight lost during exercise

Adapted from guidelines provided by the American College of Sports Medicine (ACSM)

ATHLETES AND PARENTS >> Players should take responsibility for following the guidelines on how much to drink before, during, and after games; parents should be monitoring to make sure it’s done the right way. If a player did not hydrate right ahead of time, she is not going to perform her best; if she did, and if she keeps up with her water intake during the game, she should be able to last through the whole game and perform very well.
